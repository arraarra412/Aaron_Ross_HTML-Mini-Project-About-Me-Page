Holiday Pork Conjee

Description

Vietnamese congee, or Cháo, is a comforting, savory rice porridge, simmered until creamy, often made with rich chicken or duck broth, and served with various toppings like shredded meat, ginger, herbs (rau răm), and century eggs. It's a versatile comfort food eaten for breakfast (especially to warm up), a light dinner, or as a restorative meal when ill, but it's especially popular during cooler months or Tết (Lunar New Year) as a symbol of family togetherness and a fresh start, offering simple, hearty nourishment.

I first had Conjee a few weeks ago at a Vietnamese holiday party. It was quite savory, hearty, and had a wonderful richness - cooked via a fried/baked pig head. It's a wonderful "Holiday Hangover Meal" if imbibing during the holidays is your thing. The dish reminded me of savory dishes from the Caribbean, and my family origins of Trinidad. Celebrating and sharing both culture and homeland cuisine is one of my favorite aspects of living in NYC, and was a warm welcome after living in the islands of Hawaii for two years (where pork is commonly eaten as well).

Ingredients

  • Fried/Baked/Grilled Pig Head
  • Baby Rice
  • Scallions
  • Bean Sprouts
  • Cilantro
  • Salt/Black/White Pepper
  • Fish Sauce
  • Ginger or Other Herbs

Steps

  1. Purchase or fry/grill a pig's head
  2. Bring water to a boil in a large pot
  3. Add baby rice and the pig head
  4. Add salt/pepper to taste
  5. leave on a light boil for 45 minutes
  6. Taste and test: the rice should be cooked softly and the meat on the pig's head should be falling off
  7. Try a sample adding fish sauce, cilantro, bean sprouts, scallions, salt/pepper, and any other herbs like ginger, all to taste
  8. Continue to cook rice and pig if needed
